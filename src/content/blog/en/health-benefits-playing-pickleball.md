Have you ever felt that the gym is boring or that other racket sports are too demanding on your body? If so, **pickleball** is the answer you've been looking for.

At our club in **Isla del Fraile**, we see daily how people of all ages (from 8-year-old kids to adults over 70) improve their quality of life thanks to this sport. It's not just fun; it's preventive medicine. Here are the 5 most important health benefits.

## 1. Strong heart, burned calories
Pickleball is an excellent aerobic exercise. Because points are typically longer than in tennis, you keep your heart rate in a constant "fat-burning zone." A one-hour match can help you burn between **400 and 600 calories**, almost without realizing it because you're having so much fun.

## 2. A friend to your joints
Unlike tennis or padel, where movements are long and stops are abrupt, a pickleball court is smaller. This means:
- Less pressure on your knees.
- Less impact on your lower back.
- Lower risk of overexertion injuries.
It's the ideal sport if you want to stay active but your joints are already giving you some warnings.

## 3. Goodbye to stress (The endorphin effect)
Playing outdoors in **Águilas**, with mountain and sea views in the background, is a balm for the mind. Exercise releases endorphins and serotonin, the happiness hormones. Many of our players tell us that work stress disappears the moment they step onto the court at **Isla del Fraile**.

## 4. Mental agility and coordination
Pickleball is a game of strategy. The player who hits the hardest doesn't always win, but rather the one who places the ball better. This forces your brain to stay engaged, improving your:
- Hand-eye coordination.
- Reaction time.
- Balance and reflexes.
It is, literally, a workout to keep your brain young.

## 5. The most social sport in Águilas
Loneliness is one of the biggest health issues of the 21st century. Pickleball is inherently social. Because it's played on a small court, you're close enough to your partners and opponents to joke and chat between points. At **Pickleball Águilas**, you don't just come to play; you come to be part of a family.
