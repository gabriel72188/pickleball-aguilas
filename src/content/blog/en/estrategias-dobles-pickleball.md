If you've come to play at **Pickleball Águilas Club**, you will have noticed that the vast majority of matches are played in doubles. It's the queen modality par excellence: it requires less individual physical wear but much more teamwork, communication, and team strategy.

If you want to stop losing those silly points and start dominating the court with your partner, here are 3 key concepts we teach in our classes.

## 1. Move up to the kitchen line... TOGETHER!

The number one mistake beginners make is that one player moves up to the net and the other stays at the back of the court waiting. This creates a huge diagonal gap that any opponent with a bit of cunning will exploit.

**The golden rule:** In doubles, you move as if you were tied by an imaginary rope. If your partner moves up to the non-volley zone (the kitchen), you must accompany them immediately. The team that dominates that line, dominates the match.

## 2. The third shot: The Drop Shot

In pickleball, the serving team has an initial disadvantage: they must let the opponent's return bounce before they can hit again.

The goal of your third shot (the one you hit after the return) should not be to hit a "cannonball" from the baseline. The ideal play is a soft, arcing shot that lands right in the opponents' kitchen. This will force them to hit the ball from below the net, giving you and your partner the perfect amount of time to run and position yourselves at the kitchen line.

## 3. Talk all the time

"Mine!", "Yours!", "Out!". On such a small court, half-second doubts about who is going to hit the ball are costly. Agree before the match who is going to take the balls that go down the middle (usually the player who has their dominant hand pointing to the middle of the court / forehand).
