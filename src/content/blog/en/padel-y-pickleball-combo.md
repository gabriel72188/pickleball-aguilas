If you are one of those who never miss their weekly match on the **Pádel Águilas** courts, you have probably already noticed those slightly smaller courts with a lower net that are causing a sensation: the **Pickleball** ones.

Many players ask us if they have to choose between one or the other. The answer is a resounding NO! In fact, padel players who try pickleball discover that both sports are the best allies.

## The perfect cross-training

Playing pickleball a couple of times a month will bring many benefits to your game on the padel court:

1. **Feline reflexes at the net:** In pickleball, the game in "the kitchen" zone is incredibly fast. It forces you to always have your paddle ready and to block shots at high speed. When you return to the padel court, your opponents' volleys will seem to go in slow motion!
2. **Force control:** Being played with a perforated plastic ball, pickleball requires a lot of precision and "touch". You will learn to make drop shots and soft hits that you can later apply in padel to displace your opponent.
3. **Zero walls, 100% anticipation:** Without the rebound off the glass, in pickleball you have to be very attentive to the ball's trajectory from the very first millisecond. This drastically improves your reading of the game.

## Two sports, one club

In our resort at Isla del Fraile you have the privilege of enjoying both disciplines. You can alternate your routine: a day of power and smashes in padel, and another day of reflexes and quick strategy in pickleball. 

If you already play padel, we guarantee that you will catch onto the pickleball technique in your first 5 minutes on the court. Ask us about renting pickleball paddles the next time you come to the club and dare to try the combo everyone is talking about.
